Hi, I'm Emily


I’m a writer drawn to stories where everyday life meets something darker, stranger, and quietly supernatural. My work often blends domestic realism with horror and folklore, exploring how family, history, and culture shape old stories into new ones.

I’m especially interested in creating character-driven stories where the emotional stakes feel as real as the supernatural ones. The stories I tell are the kind where the horror isn’t just the monster in the room, but the complicated love, grief, and loyalty between the people trying to survive it.

Much of my current work focuses on culturally rooted horror and magical realism. I’m developing a novel centered on two siblings navigating their grief, responsibility, and a generational curse rooted in Mexican folklore. Through stories like this, I explore themes of family, identity, and the ways the past lingers in the present.

Outside of my own creative work, I’ve worked as a writing consultant and editor, helping others develop their ideas and to strengthen their voices as writers. That experience has shaped how I think about writing, as it is not just an art but an essential tool for expression and connection.

In essence, storytelling is how I make sense of the world. Whether I’m writing fiction, for the screen, or helping someone else improve their work, I’m always looking for the same thing. A story that is honest, immersive, and a little haunting.
